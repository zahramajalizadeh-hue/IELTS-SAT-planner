Over the next 3–6 months

The roadmap

Foundation

Weeks 1–4
  • Week 1: Full SAT practice test in Bluebook + a full IELTS practice test. Write down both baseline scores.
  • Weeks 2–4: Follow the daily plan above exactly. Log every single mistake from day one — this is the data the next phase runs on.

Targeted

Weeks 5–12
  • Every 2 weeks: retake a short check — one SAT module + one IELTS skill test — to see what's actually moving.
  • Whichever skill is lagging gets an extra maintenance slot that week. Swap a slot, don't add one — keep the total time the same.

Simulation

Final 4–6 weeks
  • Every week: one full SAT test (all modules) in Bluebook + one full IELTS mock (all 4 skills), both timed exactly like exam day.
  • Review every miss. By now your error log should start predicting your mistakes before you make them.
Quick wins

Tips that actually move your score

IELTS · Listening

Skim the questions during the intro, before audio starts — predicting beats reacting.

IELTS · Reading

Find the exact sentence the answer comes from. Most mistakes are about location, not comprehension.

IELTS · Writing

At Band 7+, fully answering the question beats fancy vocabulary. Address every part of the prompt first.

IELTS · Speaking

Record every practice answer. What sounds fine in your head often sounds hesitant out loud.

SAT · Math

Build your formula sheet from your own error log, not a textbook — memorize only what you keep missing.

SAT · Reading & Writing

Eliminate two clearly wrong answers first, then decide between what's left.

Before you start

Take one full SAT practice test in Bluebook before Week 1 — it tells you whether Math or Reading/Writing needs more of your time, so you can shift the plan above accordingly.

Where to practice

Resources

IELTS

  • Cambridge IELTS books (latest volumes) — closest material to the real exam.
  • British Council — Road to IELTS — lessons tied directly to band descriptors.
  • IELTS Liz / E2 IELTS (YouTube) — band-specific feedback for Writing and Speaking.

SAT

  • Bluebook (College Board) — the official app, use it for every full-length test.
  • Khan Academy SAT Prep — personalizes practice from your Bluebook diagnostic.
  • College Board Question Bank — official released questions, sorted by skill.
